“The Geoscope is a global monitoring system that visualizes the progress of bespoke species after they have been deployed to aid threatened biomes. Through immersive projection mapping the Geoscope offers a trans-scalar view of the planet from global to microscopic, while weaving a web of interconnections among the physical specimens of the speculative species repopulating the planet. A benevolent artificial intelligence and network of human scientists and bionic droids carefully tend to the regeneration of the planet and report on their efforts to bring the world back from the brink.” - Certain Measures↗

My role in this project was on a contract basis, where I served as animator and cinematographer for ‘Geoscope’ video assets. Given a set of ficticious, engineered plant and animal species designed to resist climate disaster, I created a library of animations depicting each species in-situ to communicate how they would interact with their respective environments. Additionally, I created several live-action clips depicting scientists and researchers in the field, observing and handling various species and specimens.

Images are courtesy of Certain Measures and Museum of the Future.

Live Action

The live action content was a mix of overhead clips depicting a researcher handling samples, and a researcher in the field collecting samples from a site. Due to the resolution of the selected projectors and the scale of the projection on the wall, size and resolution of the content was a primary constraint. Actual projection size of video content would be less than 360 pixels in either direction.

Animation

Animated clips, like those below, depicted species in their respective environments. The species’ concepts, design, and 3D models were created by the Certain Measures team. My responsibilities included importing the models to Cinema4D, creating environments and lighting for each, creating any necessary rigging and/or motion, and rendering with Octane.

Exhibition

The projection is currently on display at Museum of the Future in Dubai. This project was a lot of fun to work on, and helped me hone my animation and compositing skills, while also working within an established design system.
