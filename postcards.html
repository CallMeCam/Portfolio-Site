Postcards
SCI-Arc (2020)

Video
Maya
Cinema4D
Octane

This project is a re-imagining of the postcard as a physical piece of infrastructure and advertisement. Through the creation of fictitious micro-landscapes, the postcards act as floating billboards and parks that roam througout the city.

Brief

Postcards was developed during a studio course titled "Not Making Architecture". The goal was to explore well outside the realm of conventional architecture, and even dip into the surreal. The project culminated into a short film, shown below. Models were created in Maya, scenes were established in Cinema4D, rendering was done with Octane, and compositing was completed in After Effects.

Process

The following is an incomplete glimpse into the progression of the project. Quantum computers, old hair curlers, and early x-ray were combined into collages. Following this initial exercise, I moved into three dimensions, thinking in physical space and of physical objects. I then tied these newly created forms back to earlier inspiration by incorporating hair. A bit of story development or narrative building helped ground the objects in their world, along with their pig companions. Finally, in order to figure out how they exist in our world, I landed on blimp-like advertisements of far-off places.
